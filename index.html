006 · Biomedical Design
Prosthetic Knee — Patient-Centered Design

Designed a functional above-knee prosthetic for a 6-foot male patient as part of Advanced Mechanical Engineering Design. The goal was to produce a manufacturable, real-world-viable device, not just a theoretical exercise. Material selection prioritized structural adequacy, biocompatibility, weight, and cost, mirroring constraints an actual prosthetist would face. The socket geometry accommodates residual limb loading, while the lower linkage provides controlled knee flexion and stability under gait forces. FEA validated structural integrity under expected static and dynamic loading conditions.

SolidWorks FEA Biomedical Design Material Selection DFM Human Factors
Key Design Decisions
  • Patient profile: 6 ft male, body weight loading applied as boundary condition in FEA
  • Socket designed to distribute residual limb pressure and reduce stress concentrations
  • Linkage geometry selected for natural gait kinematics and stance-phase stability
  • Material chosen for real-world manufacturability, weight, and durability trade-offs

03 / Research

Publications

Journal Article · 2026
A comparison of photoacoustic and grayscale measurements of soot deposition on surfaces
Amy E. Mensch, Ryan Falkenstein-Smith, Shine Park
Fire Safety Journal · Volume 162, July 2026, 104767 · Elsevier
Investigates non-invasive soot deposition measurement techniques as practical tools for fire investigators. Compares a novel photoacoustic method — which uses a camera flash and microphone to measure deposited soot mass — against an established grayscale image analysis approach. The photoacoustic method produced equal or higher correlation coefficients and performed consistently across drywall and metal substrates.
